Computer During War

At the onset of the Second World War there was an increase for computer projects which technical progress to develop computer to exploit their potential importance. In 1941 the German engineer Konrad Zuse developed a computer the Z3 to design airplanes and missiles, later the British completed a secret code-breaking computer to decode the German messages. Also the Electronic Numerical and computer (ENIAC) was produced by the partnership between the U.S and the University of Pennsylvania in 1943 and the computer is a massive machinery it consumed 160 kilowatts of electrical power, enough to dim the light to a section of a bigger town.
